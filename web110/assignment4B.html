Assignment 4B

The first security activity I completed was Internet Safety. This lesson taught me many important ways to stay safe while using the Internet. I learned how to create strong passwords by using a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters instead of simple words. I also learned how to recognize phishing emails, avoid spam, protect my personal information on social media, and safely shop online. Another important topic was malware, including viruses and ransomware, and how keeping software updated can reduce security risks. I also learned about browser security features, Wi-Fi security, and why it is important to verify websites before entering personal information. This activity showed me that online safety depends on making smart decisions every day. I can apply these concepts by protecting my personal accounts, using stronger passwords, enabling two-factor authentication whenever possible, and avoiding suspicious emails or websites. These habits will also help me protect future clients and business information as I continue learning web development and technology.

The second security activity I completed was Internet Security. I learned that Internet security uses different protocols to protect information while it travels across networks. I studied IPSec, which provides security at the network layer, and SSL, which protects data during communication by providing privacy, integrity, and authentication. I also learned about common cyber threats such as phishing, malware, spam, ransomware, and hacking attacks. One of the most valuable lessons was learning how phishing emails often contain poor grammar, fake links, and urgent messages designed to trick users into revealing personal information. Understanding these security threats helped me realize how important it is to verify websites and email messages before sharing sensitive information. I can apply this knowledge by recognizing online scams, protecting my devices, and using secure websites whenever I access personal or financial accounts. These skills will also be valuable in my future career because cybersecurity is an essential part of working with websites, computers, and online services.
